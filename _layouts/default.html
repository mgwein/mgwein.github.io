About Me

I am an incoming Ph.D. student in Cybernetic Systems & Artificial Intelligence at the University of Florida, advised by Dr. Alina Zare. Prior to that, I received my M.Eng. degree in Electrical & Computer Engineering from the University of Florida, while conducting research as a Graduate Research Assistant in the GatorSense Machine Learning & Sensing Lab on the NEON Macrosystems project. I also worked closely with Dr. Joel Harley in the SmartDATA Lab.

Prior, I received my B.S degree in Civil & Structural Engineering from the University of Florida and had a sucessful career as a civil engineer across many focuses, finishing in heavy industrial and marine engineering focusiing in bridge construction.

Along with this, I have 5+ years of experience in the Product/Hardware Development working on microprocessor applications, data transer compression, and QCQA manufacturing techniques. I have also interned with L3Harris as a software engineering on several secure projects.

My research interests pertain to in Computer Vision, Deep Learning, and Cybernetic Systems and their military and SAR applications, focusing on basic to advanced architectures of supervised and unsupervised deep neural networks to aid in complex task completion and advisory aspects of time orientied, strategic, physical world challenges.
